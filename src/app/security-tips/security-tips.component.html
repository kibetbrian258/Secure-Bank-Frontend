Security Tips

Protecting your financial information and accounts

At Secure Bank, we take the security of your accounts and personal information very seriously. While we employ advanced security measures to protect your data, security is a shared responsibility. Follow these essential tips to help safeguard your banking information and identity.

Account Security

Strong PIN & Password Practices

  • Create Strong Credentials

    Use unique PINs and passwords that combine letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid easily guessable information like birthdays or sequential numbers.

  • Regular Updates

    Change your PIN and online banking password every 3-6 months.

  • Unique Passwords

    Use different passwords for your banking accounts than you use for other online services.

  • Password Manager

    Consider using a reputable password manager to generate and store complex passwords securely.

Never share your PIN or password

Bank staff will never ask for your complete PIN or password. Be suspicious of any requests for these credentials.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second verification step when you log in or perform sensitive transactions.

SMS Code

A one-time code sent to your registered mobile number.

Authentication App

Generate time-based codes using an authentication app on your smartphone.

Biometric Verification

Use your fingerprint or face recognition for enhanced security.

Enable 2FA for all accounts

Go to Settings > Security in your online banking profile to enable and manage 2FA options.

Online & Mobile Banking Security

Secure Device Practices

  • Keep your computer, smartphone, and tablet operating systems up to date
  • Install and maintain reputable antivirus and anti-malware software
  • Use a secure, private internet connection for banking activities
  • Enable automatic screen lock on all your devices
  • Regularly clear your browser cache and cookies
Avoid public Wi-Fi for banking

Never conduct banking activities on public or unsecured Wi-Fi networks. Use your mobile data connection instead, or wait until you have access to a secure network.

Safe Online Banking Habits

Do

  • Type the bank's URL directly in your browser or use bookmarks
  • Look for "https://" and the padlock icon in the address bar
  • Log out completely when you finish your banking session
  • Monitor your accounts regularly for unauthorized activity
  • Use the official mobile banking app instead of the browser when on mobile devices

Don't

  • Click on links in emails or messages claiming to be from your bank
  • Leave your device unattended while logged into online banking
  • Save your banking password in your browser
  • Use the "Remember Me" option on shared or public computers
  • Download files or click on pop-ups while banking online

Fraud Awareness & Prevention

Common Scams to Watch For

Phishing

Fraudulent emails, text messages, or calls that appear to be from your bank requesting personal information or login credentials.

Red Flag: Urgent requests for personal information or threatening language

Smishing

Text messages claiming to be from your bank, often containing suspicious links or requesting sensitive information.

Red Flag: Messages about "suspicious activity" that ask you to click links

Vishing

Voice calls where scammers pose as bank representatives to extract personal or account information.

Red Flag: Callers who pressure you to act immediately or provide credentials

Fake Banking Apps

Counterfeit mobile applications designed to steal your login information and banking details.

Red Flag: Apps not downloaded from official app stores or with poor reviews
Remember

Secure Bank will never request your PIN, full password, or security codes via email, phone, or text message. We will never ask you to transfer money to a "safe account."

Card Security

Protecting Your Physical Cards

  • Guard Your Card

    Keep your card in a secure location and never leave it unattended.

  • Shield Your PIN

    Cover the keypad when entering your PIN at ATMs or payment terminals.

  • Inspect ATMs

    Check for unusual devices or attachments on ATMs that could be skimming devices.

  • Report Lost Cards

    Contact our 24/7 emergency line immediately if your card is lost or stolen.

  • Monitor Statements

    Review your card statements regularly and report any unauthorized transactions.

Safe Online Shopping

  • Only shop on secure websites (look for https:// and the padlock icon)
  • Use a credit card or virtual card number for online purchases when possible
  • Keep records of your online purchases and confirmations
  • Opt for additional authentication steps for online payments
  • Be suspicious of deals that seem too good to be true
Virtual Card Feature

Use our Virtual Card feature in the mobile app for online shopping. These temporary card numbers provide an extra layer of security for your actual account.
